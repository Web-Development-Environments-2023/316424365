    About Me


    I was born on June 1, 1937 in Memphis, Tennessee and grew up in Mississippi, where I also started my hobby of acting in school plays which later became my life's work.
    I grew up in a low income family of five children. My father was a barber, and my mother was a teacher.

    I have been married twice in my life. My first marriage was to Jeanette Adair Bradshaw, and we were together from 1967 to 1979.
    We have two children together, a son named Alfonso and a daughter named Dina. Later, I married Myrna Cooley-Lee, and we were together from 1984 to 2010.
    We adopted my step-granddaughter, Adana Haynes, who was tragically killed in 2015. My children have given me four grandchildren, and I love spending time with them.
    One of my granddaughters, Morgana Freeman, is even following in my footsteps as an actress.

    Among my many roles I am an American actor, director and narrator. I am known for my unique deep voice and I have gained my recognition from a variety of different roles
    in a wide variety of film genres in which I have taken part. Throughout my career spanning over five decades, I have received many accolades.
    Among the many awards I have been blessed with over the years, you can find an Oscar Award, a Screen Actors Guild Award and a Golden Globe Award.
    I am the recipient of the 2008 Kennedy Center Honors Award, the 2011 AFI Lifetime Achievement Award, the Cecil B. Damil for 2012 and Lifetime Achievement Award of the Screen Actors Guild for 2018.



    Some facts about me that you might not know:

    • After learning about the impact bees have on the environment and their rapid decline, I converted my farm into a bee sanctuary with 26 hives.
    • A friend and I built a prize cabinet long before I won any prize. My friend believed that someday I would win an award for acting - which eventually became a reality.
    • Freeman is a very charitable guy and has supported and created many foundations over the years. Some include education and resource aid for underprivileged children as well as assisting financially with natural disasters.

    Use Case1:
    buy a book online

    Web 1.0: The website will be static and display information about the book, such as the title, author and abstract. It is not possible to interact with the site
    or purchase the book directly. The site will not be personalized.

    Web 2.0: The site will be dynamic and allow searching and purchasing the book directly. You can interact with the site by leaving reviews, rating the book and sharing
    it on social media.

    Web 3.0: The site will be decentralized, allowing peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries such as banks or payment processors. You can interact
    with the site by leaving reviews, rating the book, etc. The site will be customized to your preferences and location, presenting recommendations based on previous purchases or searches.

    Use Case2:
    A read-only online encyclopedia site,
    a shared social media platform, and a
    decentralized social media platform

    Web 1.0: An online encyclopedia site where users can only read information but cannot contribute or edit. The content was static and provided by a central server.
    Users could only access the information but could not contribute to it or make changes.

    Web 2.0: Partially distributed. A social media platform where users can create and share content. The content is still stored on a central server owned by the platform,
    which controls access and management of the content.

    Web 3.0: Fully distributed. A decentralized social media platform where users can create and share content, and the data is stored in a decentralized network of nodes.
    The architecture is fully distributed, meaning content is stored on a network of nodes instead of a central server. This enables safer and more transparent sharing of
    information, as well as greater control and ownership of personal data.

    Use Case3:
    search a good restaurant in my area.

    Web 1.0: May suggest you visit a static website that lists restaurants in your area along with their addresses and phone numbers. Just showing you regular information.

    Web 2.0: You may visit a website that allows you to search for restaurants based on specific criteria, such as location, price range, type of cuisine, and user ratings.
    You can read reviews, leave comments and share your experiences with others.

    Web 3.0: You can use a smart personal assistant, like Siri or Alexa, to find a good restaurant for you. The assistant can understand personal preferences based on past
    interactions and personal data, such as your dietary restrictions and favorite cuisines. Takes into account real-time information, such as weather conditions.
    Uses artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms to deliver a personalized and informed experience.
